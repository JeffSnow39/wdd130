Covenant History and Founding

The Covenant empire was founded in 852 BC after a long and brutal war between the Prophets and Elites. This war was started because the Prophets were reverse engineering Forerunner technology to advance themselves. The Elites on the other hand believed this was sacrilege because they believed the artifacts were sacred and should not be touched. The Elites had better warriors but the Prophets had better technology. Eventually a truce was formed and the Prophets would tell the Elites about the Great Journey which they believed would allow them to obtain godhood. After this the Writ of Union would be signed with the Prophets as the religious leaders and incharge of reverse engineering technology, and the ELites would be their personal guard/military. Eventually they would come across other races to bring into the Covenant. In 784 the Taming of the Hunters would occur when they realized this race of giant worms that were eating Forerunner relics could be controlled and put to use. Hunters are made up of a colony of worms that form together to create something of a hivemind. They can take on numerous different forms. In 1112 the Drones would be incorporated into the Covenant. The Drones are giant insects that are really good with machines, come from a high gravity world, and are incapable of feeling fear. The only reason they didn’t get wiped out was because the Covenant thought their home world contained Forerunner artifacts, but there weren’t any. In 1342 the Jackals would be incorporated into the Covenant. Prior to this point the Jackals were comprised of merchants, pirates, and other things. They would commonly fight amongst themselves, but when the Covenant showed up they gathered together all of their ships to form a defense. This only stalled the Covenant. The Jackals don’t really care much about the Covenant faith and are mostly just used as mercenaries and the pirates are given free reign to attack whatever isn’t aligned with the Covenant. Jackals have 3 subspecies and are a matriarchy. They are armed with a shield gauntlet that blocks gun fire and are commonly used as snipers because of their excellent vision and aim. In 2142 the Grunts would be incorporated into the Covenant. The Grunts wear these backpacks that contain methane which they need to breathe. Since methane is a flammable gas they essentially have a bomb strapped to their back that they need in order to not suffocate. These are the lowest rank in the Covenant and make up the bulk of their forces. There was a Grunt rebellion that shook the Covenant and was stopped by glassing portions of their planet to make them give up and deploying an Arbiter. In 2492 the Covenant discovered the Brutes and incorporated them into their ranks. The Covenant can best be described as a militaristic theocracy with a racial caste system. They crush any descent and will make an example out of you. The Covenant call the Spartan super soldiers demons because from their perspective Chief is a literal demon. In ODST they will call you an imp which is a lesser demon. That was a cool detail that enhances them.

Weapons

Type-25 Direct Energy Pistol

Normally referred to as the Plasma Pistol, this weapon is the most commonly used side arm for the lesser Covenant species. The weapon fires as fast as you can pull the trigger, and if you hold down the trigger it will fire a charged shot that can emp shields/vehicles. In 1-2 the Plasma Pistol could hold the charged shot infinitely, but from 3 onwards it would start draining the battery as you held it down. Just like all other energy weapons the gun doesn't reload, has a limited battery and will need to be swapped out, and will overheat if fired for too long. In lore these things can cause third-degree burns, melt through metal, and if you get shot in the chest this thing will burn a hole right through you. The projectiles are green. In 2-3 they can be dual wielded.

Type-25 Direct Energy Rifle

Normally just called the Plasma Rifle, this weapon fires continueous plasma bolts that are blue colored. These are mainly used by Elites, with Brutes having their own variant, and occasionally high ranking lesser Covenant will have these. These guns will fire faster than the plasma pistol but their drawback is that they overheat faster. When fired continueously it isn't that accurate, but when fired in burst you can shoot faster and much more accurately. In 2-3 they can be dual wielded.

Type-33 Guided Munitions Launcher

Commonly referred to as the Needler, these will fire out crystal projectiles that will track their targets and if enough stick to something they will explode. Mainly used by lesser Covenant and sometines by Elites and rarer Brutes these will be easy to get your hands on. They are esecially dangerous to anyone who is unarmored or unshielded. In 2 they could be dual wielded, but due to the gun being buffed in 3 they can only can only be single wielded. In lore the Covenant don't even know how these things work. Like most of their technology they've just reverse engineered Forerunner tech to advance themselves.

Type-51 Carbine

Introduced in Halo 2 and appearing in all future games aside from Reach/Infinite. This is a singleshot precision weapon used by Brutes, Jackals, and Elites. The projectiles used by this gun are radioactive so when firing and reloading you have a chance of getting radiation poisoning. Luckily by the time Halo takes place we have better radiation treatments and cancer has been cured so this won't be as bad.

Type-1 Energy Sword

The Energy Sword is a weapon only ever used by the Elites. Due to their Honor code it must only be "unshiethed" to draw blood. The weapon consists of a handle that releases plasma gas and magnetically stablizes the blades. These things are used by high ranking Elites and can easily get through armor and shields. Think of them as being like a weirdly shaped Light Saber from Star Wars. In 1 you couldn't pick them up and they'd disintigrate. From 2 onwards the player can use them.

Type-50 Sniper Rifle System

The Beam Rifle is mostly used by Jackals because they make excellent snipers. Sometimes an Elite will be using one, but this is only when they're on the player's side. These energy weapons fire particles that tear right through flesh causing third degree burns and can easily take out shields. They will overheat if fired too frequently. They act as the Covenant's counterpart to the Sniper Rifle. In 2 Jackals armed with these would oneshot you on Legendary diffuculty because that game was super rushed and they didn't have time to properly fleshout the sandbox.

Type-33 Light Anti-Armor Weapon

The Fuel Rod fires out "globs" of fuel/jel. In 1 you couldn't pick it up and the casing was purple, while in all other games it was gold and useable. The thing can fire shots out fast, but they don't track or do as much damage as the rocket launcher. The Hunters is a variant of these weapons and will be armed with either or morter or can fire a continous beam. In lore these produce radiation and will burn right through you before exploding. Commonly used by Grunts, Brute Chieftains, and a few Elite ranks. You will want to give your allies these weapons.

Type-52 Directed Energy Support Weapon

Mainly referred as the Plasma cannon this is the Covenant counterpart to the UNSC heavy machine gun. They will be put in defensive locations. They can be broken off and carried around like the human turrets. These have 200 battery when held and infininte when mounted. Just like the human turret these things will slow you down when holding them. They can also be seen mounted on the sides of Phantoms, held by Brute Chieftians, and held by some high ranking Elites. They appeared in 3 and returned for all games going forward.

Type-1 Anti-Peronal Grenade

The Plasma Grenade first appeared in 1 and has returned for all the games. These things are the main grenade being used by most Covenant forces. They're also referred to as sticky grenades because they will stick to the enemy. They will burn bright blue and kill anything nearby.

Type-52 Pistol

The Mauler is essentially a shotgun that you carry with one hand so they can be dual wielded. First introduced in Halo 3 since the Brutes replaced the Elites these are mainly used by Brute Bodyguards and the Stalker rank. This kind of weapon is hard for normal humans to hold because it's heavy and really powerful. Just like most Brute weapons this thing has a blade at the end for increased melee damage.

Type-25 Carbine

The Spiker gets it's name from the fact that these sigle-handed machine guns fire superheated spikes that tear through flesh. It has a 40 round mag, has blades at the end, and can be dual wielded. This is the main weapon used by the Brutes. They only appeared in 3, ODST, and Reach

Type-25 Grenade Launcher

First appearing in Halo 2, the Bruteshot is one of the staple weapons of the Brutes. This is mainly used by the higher ranking Brutes in a pack. In 2 the grenades would bounce off surfaces but in 3 and the other games they would just detinate upon hitting a surface. This has a large blade and does double normal melee damage. In earleir builds of 2 they would fire what looked like electricity balls. Luckily this was changed making the weapon stand out.

Type-2 Energy Weapon/Hammer

The Gravity Hammer was first introduced in 2 as a unique weapon used only by the Brte Tartaras. In 3 it got a redesign and is now used by Brute Chieftains. When slaming it down it releases a pulse that slams enemies and objects in all directions. This unique Brute melee weapon adds a great contrast between them and the Elites. They appear in 2 and all other games.

Type-2 Anti-Personal Fragmentation Grenade

The Spike Grenades are the Brute equivlant to the standard Plasma Grenades. In addition to being able to stick to players/enemies these can stick to any surface. The blast doesn't do much damage, but it makes up for that by sending out the same spikes used by the Spikers. These are meant to impale people in the immediate area. They were only in 3, ODST, and Infinite.

Type-3 Anti-Personal Incendiary Grenade

Firebomb grenades were first introduced in 3 and only returned in ODST. These are mainly used by the Brute Stalkers. They will light anything on fire and leave flames on the ground. They have an incindiary gel that ignites when reacting with the air. They can do a lot of damage to infantry and even vehicles.

Type-52 Guided Munitions Launcher/Explosive

The Plasma Launcher was implimented in Reach and was in 5 as a glitch. This weapon was used before and during the Human Covenant War. The weapon fires ammunition similar to the plasma grenades that stick to their target. The projectiles will track their target with a red circle appearing in screen showing the weapon spotted it's target. It must first charge up before it can fire. This weapon is mainly used by Elites, but has also been used by high ranking Grunts and Brutes. The weapon's model appeared in 4, but the weapon itself was unusable.

Type-51 Directed Energy Rifle/Improved

In terms of functionality the Plasma Repeater is basically a bigger more powerful version of the Plasma Rifle. It is used by Elites and Brutes in combat. It is believed that this weapon was a relativly recent creation or just really rare because it was rarely seen outside of certian groups. Unlike most plasma weapon this gun doesn't overheat because it has flaps that can be manually opened. It just becomes more inaccurate whenheating up. When fired in bursts it is more effective. The gun would become widespread after the war. There was even going to be a Brute version in Halo Wars 2, but that sadly didn't happen. The plasma fired has the same blue color as the Elite Plasma Rifle.

Type-50 Directed Energy Rifle/Heavy

The Concussion Rifles have a battery that can fire 6 plasma shots before reloading. The plasma fires a concuossive blast that functions like a grenade launcher or handheld morter. They were first used in Reach and returned in 4. Their design would be the basis for the Plasma Casters seen in 5. These weapons were mainly used by high ranking Elites and Brute shock troops. In Reach the Phantom and Spirit dropships fire these same projectiles from their turrets.

Type-52 Special Applications Rifle

Unlike most Covenant technology that is just reverse engineered Forerunner technology these weapons are an example of Covenant innovation. This has a similar design to the Beam Rifle, but instead of being meant to take out targets quickly it is meant to suppress the enemy. The disadvantage this weapon has is the beam is visible to anyone looking, the battery dies fast, and it isn't effective against vehicles or armor. Because of these drawbacks the weapon is rare and mainly just used by Jackal snipers, Skirmishers, and Elites.

Type-31 Needle Rifle

Only appearing in Reach the Needle Rifle is a fast firing precision weapon that can exlode enemies if enough needles hit their target. Unlike the Needler they don't track their target and just fire in a straight line. They are used by Elites, Brutes, and Jackals. This is the Covenant equivlent to the DMR, functions like the Carbine but with explosive rounds, and is very effective against unshielded targets. In 5 there was a Carbine variant that was functionally identical to the Needle Rifle firing out needles that explode. This is also used by the Jackal subspecies the "Skirmishers".

Type-25 Directed Energy Rifle/Jiralhanae Variant

The Brute Plasma Rifle has a faster firing rate than the normal Elite version, but it overheats faster and has a less efficient battery. Only ever seen used by Brutes and one Drone rank. This weapon has appeared in 2, ODST, and 5 as a multiplayer weapon variant. They have a red casing and fire red plasma. They were given to the Brutes during their rise through the Covenant and the Great Schisim.
