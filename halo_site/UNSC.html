UNSC History

The UNSC was formed in 2163 as a response to the resurgence of Fascist and Communist powers that were spreading into the solar system. There were a few wars that led to the UN using this organization as their military. At the time there was also the UIG United Earth Government which was formed by the UN absorbing all other nations for stability. WHen colonizing different worlds the different countries would send out ships to colonize these planets. Sometime before the Human-Covenant War there were rebellions here and there because the outer colonies didn’t have nearly as much say in what happened as the inner colonies did. The planets closer to earth had better industry, more power, and infrastructure. While the outer colonies mostly just mined resources and sent them back to the heart of human space. They felt they were unrepresented and wanted to control themselves or at least just have more industry and power. The Spartan 2 program would be formed as a way to put down these rebellions so they couldn’t threaten the power/stability of the inner colonies. When the Human-Covenant Wars broke out the colonial militia would be absorbed into the UNSC, and the organization would gain more power over what happened. Late into the war the UNSC was the government for humanity out of necessity.

Weapons

M6G Magnum

Commonly referred to as the magnum or simply pistol. This weapon and many others were produced by Misriah Armory. This and other side arms can be dual wielded. The thing fires a .50 calibre round and has a magizine size of 8 bullets. This definitly sounds like overkill but when you consider the fact that they're fighting enemeies that have regenerating shields and strong armor it's entirely nessecary. Unfortunatly it's not nearly as useful in later games as it was in the first. It was in Halos 1, 3, Reach, 4, and 5. Single shot weapons like this one will fill the role of a precision weapon.

M7 Submachine Gun

Commonly referred to ingame and by the players as the SMG this weapon can be dual wielded and is very effective at close range. In Halo 2 it acted as the replacement to the Assault Rifle and showed off the new dual wielding feature Bungie added. The gun only appears in 2, 3, and would eventually return in 5. In the games it has a crazy recoil causing your reticle to raise upwards. In universe this wouldn't really make sense because being a super soldeir you can pick up and use machine gun turrets, dual wield, and highjack vehicles. If it was like this in the lore normal humans would not be able to use it. This is purely for the purposes of gameplay. The weapon fires 5x23mm caseless rounds with a magizine size of 60 bullets.

MA5C Assault RIfle

Usually just called the Assault Rifle, this firearm has a 32 round magizine that fires out 7.62x51mm bullets. It functions the replacement to the MA5B from the first game which fired 60 rounds per magizine. Unfortunatly in terms of functionality it shares the same niche as the SMGs because it has bad accuracy and does similar damage. Later games would decrees the projectile spread and bump the magizine up to 36 rounds. The thing is a staple weapon from the Halo series, but you'll be better off replacing it once you find something better.

BR55 Battle Rifle

In game the Battle Rifle is the designated precision weapon for the humans. Like all precision weapons it can take out unshielded players/enemies with a headshot. The weapon fires a 3 round burst, and in earlier builds of the game fired a single shot. The bullets used are 9.5x40mm with a magizine size of 36. This is one of the weapons people will imedietly recognize from the games.

M90 Shotgun

The shotgun has changed quite a bit in terms of gameplay. In the first game it had really good range and was super useful. From 2 onwards it followed the unfortunate trend in games where shotguns have the effective range of a spear, and become useless due to an insane projectile spread. This is there for balancing reasons because something that can kill in 1-2 shots would be too overpowered if it was good at range. This weapon can hold 8 shells inside it and fires 8-gauge shells. Most other weapons reload the entire magazine at once but this does one shell at a time. It is your best friend when fighting the Flood because it tears them up and they'll charge you. Other than having glowing lights on the sights it looks like something that could exist now.

SRS99D-SR Sniper Rifle

The sniper is one of the most effective weapons in the game being able to kill unshielded enemies in one hit anywhere on their body. To balance this out the reticle is small and you don't get it very often. In 1 it could fire all 4 shots pretty fast, but all games going forward would reduce the firing speed. ANother thing that changed from the first game was the scope could use nightvision. This thing is catagorized as an anti-personnel weapon firing out 14.114mm rounds and having for shots per magazine.

M41 Rocket Launcher

The Rocket Launcher can hold 2 102mm rockets per mag. In 2 and Reach the thing would lock onto vehicles and fire a homing shot. For whatever reason the other games don't do this. Interestingly this gun was used in Bungie's first fps Marathon and had the same design/function. This thing is mainly used against vehicles but can also oneshot most normal enemies. If you're driving a vehicle you will want to give this and other "power weapons" to your allies because they're a better shot and have infinite ammo. It's really fun to have a bunch of guys all armed with great weapons and just go to town.

M6 G/GNR Spartan Laser

The Spartan Laser was first introduced in 3 and appeared in games Reach, ODST, 4, and 5. It basically acts as an anti everything gun because when it charges up it will send out a beam that oneshots most things. The full name for this weapon is the Weapon/Anti-Vehicle Model 6 Grindell/Galilean Nonlinear Rifle. it is the only energy weapon used by the USNC. Based on the name you'd think that only Spartans would be able to use it. Normal marines and ODSTs can hold it. The reason they call it the "Spartan Laser" is because it's super heavy and is so powerful. It has a really good range and once the laser fires up it will instantly hit whatever it's pointing at.

AIE-486H Heavy Machine Gun

Also, knows as the machine gun or HMG this mounted weapon is always found in highly defensive positions. It appeared in 3/ODST, 4, 5, Reach, and Infinite. The gun is loaded with 200 bullets and uses the same 7.62x51mm ammo as the Assault Rifle. When the thing is mounted you can fire it never runs out of ammo, but when you break it off it only carries 200 rounds. The reason for this is because it shreds infantry and vehicles. If it never ran out or had a ammo count it would be too over powered. The thing also reduces your walking speed to half because you're carrying something heavy.

LAU-65D/SGM-151 Missle Pod

The missle pod fires rockets that lock onto vehicles. When mounted just like the machine gun it can fire forever, but when not mounted it only holds 8 shots because it's so powerful. Usually you'll see these mounted in defensive positions, and first encounter them on the level where you fight a Scarab. These rockets are extremely useful against air vehicles.

M7075 Flamethrower

Introduced in 3, the flamethrower is a heavy weapon the slows you down when using it and roasts everything it catches on fire. It can light the floor, objects, and walls on fire for a short time. Also known as the M7057/Defoliant Projector this can be used to render bodies unusable for Flood consumption and can burn back the infection from taking over other hosts. It carries 100 fuel and can't be reloaded just like the other heavy weapons. In the first game you were suposed to get a flamethrower but it was cut because the first game had an interesting development cycle where the game changed radically throught development. There was suposed to be a Covenant flamethrower called the Disintigrator in 2 but that was scrapped as well. The fuel this weapon uses is Pyrosene-V.

M9 Fragmentation Grenade

The human grenades act as your standard grenades in Halo. Both Marines and ODSTs will be armed with them and throw them during gameplay. Their full name is the M9 High-Explosive Dual-Purpose Granade. Common names include Granade, Fragmentation Grenade, and Frags. In lore and gameplay they function similar to how real granades function. In some games you can carry four at once, in others that have more than two granade types it's just two, and in others it's three of each. This was done for balancing reasons. In the first game these things were super strong and would kill instantly. In other games they would be weakened.

M6C/SOCOM

Usually just referred to as the Automag, the M6S is a supressed version of the Magnum/Pistol. In terms of gameplay the gun does less damage, fires faster, and can still do headshots. Despite the fact that it's supressed firing the weapon still alerts enemies. It just gives the illusion of stealth. It fires the same ammo as the normal magnum. These will be given to spec-ops teams like ODSTs and other groups. It is meant to keep the user from being located by the enemy. Interestingly in real life a supressed weapon doesn't actually make it near silent like in games/movies. Those will reduce the sound of a gunshot from an entire block to a only being heard by a house. It will still get people's attention. It's just not as noticable.

M7S Caseless Submachine Gun

The silenced smg is given to ODSTs and other spec-ops units. Lorewise it makes the gun quieter, but in gameplay it does not. The thing fires the same 5x23mm as the normal smg. This weapon is more accurate than the normal smg and will be your main weapon in ODST.

M392 Designated Marksman Rifle

The DMR is a singleshot precision weapon that acts as the precursor to the Battle Rifle. It fires the same 7.62x51mm rounds as the Assault Rifle and has a mag size of 15. It is mainly used by the Army branch of the UNSC which explains why you don't see it in 1-3/ODST. It would reappeer in furture games. In Reach it has this annoying quirk where if you fire it too fast the gun will become wildly inaccurate. This was changed in other games.

M1319 Individual Grenade Launcher

The grenade launcher is another of many weapons produced by Misriah Armory, and is used mainly by the Army branch of the UNSC. The weapon is great for taking out groups, but isn't as good for taking out enemies/players with shields. The gun fires a single shot before reloading and uses a 40mm explosive shell. If you just pull the trigger the grenage will bounce and exploade, but if you hold down the trigger it won't explode until you release it and will emp shields/vehicles in the area. It was added in Reach and would later reappear in 5 with a few variants.

H-165 Forward Observer Module

The Target Designator is a tool used to designate targets for aircraft and spaceships in the area to bombard. In gameplay the thing only has 3 shots which will most likely be used on vehicles by the player since they're the most immediate threat. To lock onto something you will need to point the designator at a target then hold down the trigger for it to charge up. Then the heavy heavy ordinance will be dropped on the enemy. Interestingly the weapon was only availible in campaign and Firefight. After the war this would be replaced with the H-295 FOM, which functions similarly.
